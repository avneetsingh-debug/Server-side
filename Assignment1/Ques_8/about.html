About Institute of Engineering & Technology (IET-DAVV)

Devi Ahilya University, Indore has been a leading university of Central India. Within the framework of its University Teaching Departments (UTD), the University opened a self-financing engineering institute named Institute of Engineering & Technology (IET).

IET started its academic journey with glorious dreams on 04 September 1996 and is popularly known as IET-DAVV.

Initially, IET was started with a total intake of 90 students in three branches of Bachelor of Engineering (BE) – Mechanical Engineering, Computer Engineering and Electronics & Instrumentation Engineering, having 30 students each. The first batch attended classes at the School of Computer Science (SCS) and received financial support from the International Institute of Professional Studies (IIPS).

The institute is approved by All India Council for Technical Education (AICTE), New Delhi, Government of Madhya Pradesh and University Grants Commission (UGC). IET is an academically autonomous institute with a flexible and modern curriculum. It is a fully self-financing institute offering BE programmes in six branches. Recently, AICTE approved 180 additional seats, making the total intake 540 students per year.

IET became a Postgraduate Institute in 2002 and currently conducts ME programmes with six specializations. In 2006, the MSc programme in Applied Mathematics was also introduced.

PhD programmes are offered in all disciplines of BE and ME programmes and in all relevant research areas. Research activities at IET are strong, with research scholars from reputed organizations such as SGSITS, AICTE, RRCAT, NRCS, and IIITA enrolled in various departments. All UG, PG and Doctoral programmes come under the Faculty of Engineering of DAVV.
